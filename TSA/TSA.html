Time Series Introduction:¶

A Time Series is a series of data points ordered in time where time is often the independent variable

Time Series Analysis comprises methods for analyzing time series data to extract meaningful statistics and other features of the data

Time Series Forecasting is the use of a model to predict future values based on previously observed values

Time Series Models:

There are many ways to model a time series to make predictions i-e Moving Average, Exponential Smoothing etc.

Three broad classes of practical importance are the

1. The Autoregressive (AR) Models:

An autoregressive (AR) model predicts future behavior based on past behavior. The process is basically a linear regression of the data in the current series against one or more past values in the same series

2. The Integrated (I) Models:

The order of integration, denoted I(d), of a time series is the minimum number of differences required to obtain a stationary series

3. The Moving Average (MA) Models:

MA model depends on the errors(residuals) of the previous forecasts to make current forecasts

Combinations of these Models produce autoregressive moving average (ARMA) and autoregressive integrated moving average (ARIMA) models etc.

Component of Time Series:

Also know as ETS :

E refers to Error (unsystematic, short term fluctuations),

T refers to Trend(long term direction) and

S refers to Seasonality(systematic, calendar related movements).

Lastly, a cyclical pattern is a result of some external factors which make them much harder to predict. Cycles do not have fixed periods, they may range from six to ten years

Stationary Time Series:

A time series is said to be stationary if it has constant mean and variance, and covariance is independent of time.

Project Overview¶

We used the restaurant historical booking orders data to show how to construct sales forecasting models to forecast future bookings. Exploratory analysis is performed by analyzing data over different interval of time to understand Trend, Seasonality and Error component. We further decomposed Time Series components to have a better idea of ETS behavior. We also covered the basic issues related to time series modeling, such as stationarity etc. We divided data into train and test datasets, Model is being fit to train data and evaluated against hold out sample i-e Test data. Forecasting is done for the next 6 months bookings and plotted next to historical data.

Testing Hypothesis if Data is Stationary¶

Since we have observed that our data is non stationary. We can perform a hypothesis testing to confirm if data is stationary or not.

Lets perform Augmented Dickey-Fuller Test to see if data is stationary or not.

If p-value is less than 0.05, then our data is stationary

Model Selection¶

Now we have concluded that our series is non stationary with error, trend and seasonality. ARIMA is a good choice to fit that kind of data. Since our data has seasonality , we will use seasonal ARIMA, also knows as SARIMA that supports the direct modeling of the seasonal component of the series.

Lets briefly describe SARIMA Models

SARIMA is specified as SARIMA (p, d q)(P, D, Q)m :

p is the number of time lags i-e an AR part

d is the degree of differencing

q is the order of Moving average model.

P is Seasonal autoregressive order.

D is Seasonal difference order.

Q is Seasonal moving average order.

m is The number of time steps for a single seasonal period

There are two ways to decide the order of p d q

Method 1:

We can decide p d q by reading the ACF and PACF plots, which is little difficult to read sometimes

Method 2:

Grid Search (i-e try different combination of SARIMA model and compare on some evaluation metrics)

Plotting ACF and PACF¶

ACF Plot:

The ACF plot shows correlation of the series with itself,lagged by x time units, in general there is a decline (gradual or sharp)

Identification of MA model is often best done with the ACF rather than the PACF, if there is a gradual decline it suggest an MA model

PACF Plot:

The PACF only describes the direct relationship between an observation and its lag.

Identification of an AR model is often best done with PACF. Typically, a sharp drop after lag k suggests an AR-k model should be used.

Lets create ACF and PACF plots

Using Grid Search¶

Grid Search is a most effective and recommended way to decide the parameters of our model as it uses AIC (Akaike Information Criterion) and BIC(Bayesian Information Criterion) as a metric to compare the performance of various ARIMA based models.

Train Test Split¶

We typically keep most recent n values as a hold out sample or test, and number of test periods should match the length of the forecast periods, i-e n test values to predict n future values.

We want to forecast the hotel booking for next 6 month, then our test data should have at least 6 observation.

Conclusion:¶

Seasonal ARIMA perform very well when working with a time series where data is directly related to the time stamp, such as airline passenger data set, hotel booking for a resort etc as we see clear growth and seasonality based on time.However, ARIMA is not suitable for stock price data as it has so many outside factors than the time stamp),just knowing the stock price over time isn't actually enough to predict stock price.
